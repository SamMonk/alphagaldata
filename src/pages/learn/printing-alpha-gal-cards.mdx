Use these instructions to print durable wallet cards, dining cards, and temporary medical alert tattoos. You can print at home or send the files to a local or online print service.

## Gather supplies

- Heavy 80 lb cover stock or pre-scored business card paper for the wallet card.
- 4x6 inch index cards or photo paper for the dining card.
- A sharp paper trimmer or scissors, plus laminating sleeves if you want extra durability.

## Print at home

1. Download the PDFs below so you can print the original resolution files.
2. Set your printer to landscape orientation and 100% scale (no fit-to-page).
3. Print a test sheet on plain paper to check alignment before moving to heavier stock.
4. Trim along the guides, fold the wallet card, and laminate if desired.

## Send to a print service

1. Share the PDF with your provider. Mention that the wallet card should stay true credit-card size and the dining card should remain 4x6 inches.
2. Ask for matte or satin finishes so you can write on the back.
3. Request a few spares so you can stage cards in travel bags and vehicles.

## Print temporary tattoos at home

1. Purchase **inkjet temporary tattoo paper** (available on Amazon, typically under $10 for 5-10 sheets).
2. Download the [wrist tattoo SVG](/downloads/ags_wrist_tattoo_simple.svg) or [full tattoo SVG](/downloads/ags_medical_alert_tattoo.svg).
3. Open the SVG in any image editor or browser, print at 100% scale on the tattoo paper.
4. Follow the tattoo paper instructions: cut out the design, apply to clean dry skin with a damp cloth, hold for 30 seconds.
5. The tattoo lasts 2-5 days. Apply to the inner wrist where EMTs check for medical alerts.
