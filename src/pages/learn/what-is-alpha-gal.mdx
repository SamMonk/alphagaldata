# What is Alpha-Gal Syndrome?

Alpha-gal syndrome (AGS) is a <IconBrandTick className="inline-block align-text-bottom" size={18}/> tick-associated allergy to a carbohydrate (galactose‑α‑1,3‑galactose) found in most mammals. Some people experience delayed reactions (often 3–6 hours) after eating mammalian products. Severity varies by person. This site is educational and not medical advice—please consult a board‑certified allergist.
